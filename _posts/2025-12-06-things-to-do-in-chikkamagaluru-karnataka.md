Chikkamagaluru, also known as the "Coffee Land of Karnataka," is a serene hill station nestled in the Western Ghats. 

Famous for its lush green coffee plantations, misty mountains, and breathtaking landscapes, it's a perfect retreat for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

## How to Reach Chikkamagaluru

By Air: The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport (IXE), about 150 km away. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a bus.
By Train: The closest railway stations are in Kadur (40 km) and Hassan (60 km). You can find taxis and buses from these stations to reach Chikkamagaluru.
By Road: Chikkamagaluru is well-connected by road to major cities like Bengaluru, Mangalore, and Mysuru. KSRTC buses and private vehicles are readily available.

## 2-Day Itinerary for Chikkamagaluru

### Day 1: Peaks and Waterfalls

Arrive in Chikkamagaluru and check into your homestay or resort.
Head to Mullayanagiri, the highest peak in Karnataka. You can either trek to the top or hire a jeep for a thrilling ride to enjoy panoramic views.
Afterward, visit Jhari Waterfalls, also known as Buttermilk Falls, a beautiful cascade nestled amidst dense forests.
In the evening, take a leisurely walk through a coffee plantation and enjoy the tranquil ambiance.

### Day 2: Coffee Culture and Scenic Lakes

Start your day with a visit to the Coffee Museum to learn about the history and process of coffee making.
Explore Baba Budangiri, a sacred mountain range for both Hindus and Muslims, known for its shrine and stunning vistas.
In the afternoon, relax by the serene Hirekolale Lake, a man-made lake surrounded by hills, offering a picturesque sunset view.
Conclude your trip with some local coffee tasting and shopping for freshly ground coffee powder.

## Tips for Visiting Chikkamagaluru:

The best time to visit is from September to May when the weather is pleasant.
Wear comfortable shoes, especially if you plan to trek.
Book your accommodation in advance, particularly during peak season and weekends.
Hiring a local jeep is recommended for visiting Mullayanagiri and other remote spots as the roads can be narrow and steep.
Don't forget to carry a light jacket as the evenings can get chilly.
