Yelagiri Hills, a serene hill station in the Tirupattur district of Tamil Nadu, is a perfect getaway for those seeking a quiet retreat from the bustle of city life. 

Unlike more commercialized hill stations, Yelagiri retains a rustic charm with its winding roads, lush greenery, and pleasant weather year-round. It is a cluster of 14 villages nestled amidst four hills, offering a unique blend of nature, adventure, and tranquility.

## How to Reach Yelagiri Hills

By Air: The nearest airports are Kempegowda International Airport in Bengaluru (BLR) and Chennai International Airport (MAA). From either airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Yelagiri.
By Train: The closest railway station is Jolarpettai Junction (JTJ), which is well-connected to major cities like Chennai, Bengaluru, and Coimbatore. From Jolarpettai, Yelagiri is about 21 km away and can be reached via local buses or taxis.
By Road: Yelagiri is well-connected by road. It is about 160 km from Bengaluru and 230 km from Chennai. The drive up the hills features 14 hairpin bends, each named after a prominent Tamil personality, offering panoramic views of the plains below. **If you are driving from Bengaluru, a stop at the Krishnagiri Dam (KRP Dam) is highly recommended. It is a beautiful spot to stretch your legs and enjoy the reservoir views before starting the climb to Yelagiri.**

## 3-Day Itinerary for Yelagiri Hills

### Day 1: Lakeside Leisure and Exotic Encounters

Arrive at Yelagiri Hills and check into your resort or homestay.
Start your day with a visit to Punganoor Lake and Park. This man-made lake is the heart of the hill station. You can enjoy a peaceful boat ride or take a leisurely walk along the paved path surrounding the lake.
Adjacent to the lake is the Nature Park, which houses a variety of plant species, a musical fountain, and a small bamboo house.
In the afternoon, head to **Fundera Park**, an absolute treat for animal lovers. Unlike a traditional zoo, this is an interactive park where you can get up close with exotic species. You can pet unique animals like **hedgehogs and iguanas**, and participate in feeding sessions with **birds, rabbits, and hamsters**. It is a fantastic hands-on experience that children and adults alike will cherish.
End your day with a relaxing evening at one of the many local cafes.

### Day 2: Trekking and Waterfalls

In the morning, head to the Swamimalai Trek. This is the highest point in Yelagiri and offers a moderate trekking experience. The trail leads to a summit with breathtaking views of the entire valley.
After the trek, drive down towards Jalagamparai Waterfalls. Located about 5 km from the main town, the Attaru River flows through the hills and drops as a beautiful waterfall. The trek to the falls is scenic and refreshing.
Visit the Velavan Temple (Murugan Temple) located on a hilltop. The temple offers a panoramic view of the Yelagiri valley and is a great spot to catch the sunset.

### Day 3: Science and Serenity

On your final day, take a short trip (about 35 km) to the **Vainu Bappu Observatory** in Kavalur. Known as one of the premier astronomical observatories in Asia, it offers a fascinating "live" experience of how an observatory functions. While it is primarily a research facility, they often have public viewing sessions (especially on Saturday nights) where you can peer through telescopes and learn about the cosmos.
On your way back, visit Nilavoor Lake, another tranquil spot where you can enjoy boating or simply relax by the water.
Before heading back, explore the local markets for pure honey, jackfruit, and other forest produce that Yelagiri is famous for.

## Tips for Visiting Yelagiri Hills:

The best time to visit is between November and February, although the weather is pleasant throughout the year.
Yelagiri is known for its pure mountain honey; make sure to buy some from the local cooperative stores.
Pack a light jacket or sweater even in summer, as the evenings can get quite cool.
The hairpin bends are narrow, so drive carefully if you are bringing your own vehicle.
Limited ATMs are available, so it's advisable to carry sufficient cash.
