Shivamogga, often called the "Gateway to Malnad," is a treasure trove of natural beauty in Karnataka. 

Blessed with lush green forests, roaring waterfalls, and rich biodiversity, it serves as a perfect destination for those looking to immerse themselves in nature.

## How to Reach Shivamogga

By Air: The nearest major airport is Mangalore International Airport (IXE), around 195 km away. Shivamogga also has its own domestic airport (RQY) with limited connectivity.
By Train: Shivamogga Town Railway Station (SMET) is well-connected to Bengaluru and other major cities in Karnataka.
By Road: The city is well-linked by an extensive network of roads. Regular government and private buses run from Bengaluru, Mangalore, and Hubli.

## 3-Day Itinerary for Shivamogga

### Day 1: The Majestic Jog Falls

Arrive in Shivamogga and check into your hotel.
After settling in, embark on a scenic drive to the world-famous Jog Falls, one of India's highest waterfalls. Witness the breathtaking sight of the Sharavathi River splitting into four cascades: Raja, Rani, Roarer, and Rocket.
On your way back, you can visit the Linganamakki Dam, which offers a panoramic view of the reservoir.
Return to Shivamogga for an overnight stay.

### Day 2: Wildlife and Avian Wonders

Start your day with a visit to the Sakrebailu Elephant Camp, located on the banks of the River Tunga, where you can watch elephants being bathed and fed.
Next, head to the Mandagadde Bird Sanctuary, a small island on the river that attracts a variety of migratory birds like median egret and darter.
In the afternoon, experience the Tyavarekoppa Lion and Tiger Safari for a chance to see these majestic animals in a semi-natural habitat.

### Day 3: History and Scenic Beauty

Explore the historical Keladi and Ikkeri temples, known for their unique Dravidian and Hoysala architectural styles.
Later, drive to Agumbe, famous for its dense rainforests and spectacular sunset views. The journey through the winding ghat roads is an experience in itself.
Visit the Agumbe Sunset View Point to witness a mesmerizing sunset over the Western Ghats before heading back.

## Tips for Visiting Shivamogga:

The best time to visit is from June to October, during and right after the monsoon, to see the waterfalls in their full splendor.
Carry rain gear and waterproof bags if you are traveling during the monsoon season.
It's advisable to check the timings for the elephant camp and safari in advance.
The roads to Jog Falls and Agumbe are scenic but can be winding. Drive cautiously.
Book accommodations in advance, especially during the peak tourist season.
