Horsley Hills, a serene hill station in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, is a quiet haven perfect for those seeking to escape the heat and chaos of the plains. 

Located at an altitude of 1,290 meters, this charming destination is fondly referred to as the "Ooty of Andhra Pradesh." Named after W.D. Horsley, a British collector who built his summer residence here in the late 19th century, the hills boast dense forests, pleasant weather, and panoramic views of the Eastern Ghats. The route up the hill is incredibly scenic, lined with eucalyptus, jacaranda, and mahogany trees, making it a favorite spot for road trippers and nature lovers alike.

## How to Reach Horsley Hills

By Air: The nearest airport is Kempegowda International Airport in Bengaluru (BLR), located about 135 km away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or catch a bus to reach Horsley Hills via Madanapalle.
By Train: The closest railway station is Madanapalle Road (MPL), situated about 26 km from the hills. However, for better connectivity, Bengaluru (SBC/YPR) or Tirupati (TPTY) stations are recommended, from where you can take a taxi or bus.
By Road: Horsley Hills is well-connected by road networks. It is approximately 140 km from Bengaluru, 270 km from Chennai, and 120 km from Tirupati. The drive up the hills is spectacular, featuring a smooth, winding ghat road with lush green bends.

## 3-Day Itinerary for Horsley Hills

### Day 1: Eucalyptus Groves and Scenic Vistas

Arrive at Horsley Hills and check into your resort or cottage.
Start your exploration with a visit to the famous **Kalyani Eucalyptus Tree**. Planted in 1859 by W.D. Horsley himself, this giant tree is over 160 years old and stands as a magnificent monument of nature.
Spend your afternoon visiting the **Forest Development Corporation Museum** and the adjacent **Mini Zoo**, which houses a variety of birds, crocodiles, and peacock displays, offering a pleasant walk in nature.
In the evening, head to the main view points near the Governor's Bungalow. Enjoy a breathtaking sunset as the sun dips below the rugged valleys, turning the sky into hues of crimson and gold.
Wrap up your day with a quiet dinner at your resort.

### Day 2: Windy Heights and Forest Trails

Wake up early and make your way to **Gali Bandalu (Wind Rocks)**. This massive rocky slope is known for the strong, gusty winds that blow across it all day. Standing here gives you an uninterrupted 360-degree view of the vast plains below.
Post breakfast, try some adventure sports like zorbing, crossing rope bridges, or ziplining, which are popular activities around the hill resort area.
In the afternoon, visit the serene **Mallamma Temple**. Dedicated to a legendary girl named Mallamma who healed the local tribes, this peaceful shrine offers a tranquil environment for reflection.
Later, take a gentle trek or a forest trail walk through the dense paths of the hills, spotting local flora and listening to the calls of various hill birds.

### Day 3: Cascading Falls and Departure

On your final day, check out of your accommodation and drive down towards the scenic **Kaigal Falls** (also known as Dumukurallu Waterfalls), located about 30 km away. The waterfall cascades from a height of 40 feet into a natural pool, surrounded by dense sanctuary forests. It is an ideal spot to relax before heading home.
On your way back, stop by the local cooperative stores to buy pure, locally harvested forest honey and eucalyptus oil.
Depart for your onward journey with beautiful memories of the hills.

## Tips for Visiting Horsley Hills:

The best time to visit is from September to February when the weather is cool and the hills are often enveloped in mist.
Since accommodation options on the hills are limited, booking your stay at the Haritha Hill Resort in advance is highly recommended.
Carry light jackets or sweaters as the nights and early mornings can get surprisingly cold.
Drive carefully on the ghat road, keeping an eye out for hairpin turns and occasional wildlife like monkeys.
Carry some cash as internet connectivity can be spotty, affecting digital payment modes.
